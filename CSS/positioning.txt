positioning in css

position: "value";
1. static (default)
2. relative
3. fixed
4. sticky
5. absolute

relative:

position: relative; (top, right, bottom, left)
it doesn't let any content in its original place even after changing its+'s own position

left: 50px; moves 50px away from the left
(from its initial position i.e., relative to its own position)

fixed:

position: fixed;
(mostly for navigation bars, the content goes beneath it, relative to the browser)
top : 0px;
(moves 0px away from the top of the browser)

sticky:

position: sticky;

top: 0px; (gets sticky to the window as it is 0px away from the top)

absolute:

position: absolute;

(ancestor position: relative, sticky, fixed, it changes its position relative to it's ancestor or else, in case without any ancestors, then it positions itself with the window).

z-index: 10;(0)
it would appear on top of elements if they are hidden.

overrides whichever z-index in greater
